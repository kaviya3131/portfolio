🎓 Master Thesis · In Progress · DFKI
Language-Aware Multilingual BERT: Explicit Language Conditioning for Encoder Models
Proposed and validated a novel sentence-level language conditioning approach for BERT-style encoders - the first systematic study of explicit language tokens in encoder-only models for low-resource and cross-lingual understanding tasks. Trained 6 model variants (3 from-scratch BERT-base + 3 XLM-R continued pretraining) on 1B+ tokens across 8 languages with training scaled up to 1M+ steps, achieving +9% XNLI accuracy and +9.8pp NER F1 over baselines. Engineered a custom 50K BPE tokenizer - 2× smaller than mBERT with superior efficiency on Indic scripts - and a zero-shot centroid-based language ID system reaching 92.7% accuracy (vs 12.5% baseline). Ablation studies revealed language tokens function as learned conditioning signals rather than predictable labels. Currently scaling to 50 languages for stronger cross-lingual generalisation results.
